19.1 Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, students will be able to:

  1. Restate the conceptual arc of Sessions 1–8 in their own words: diagnosis → measurement → requirements.
  2. Demonstrate proficiency in computing all four midterm question types (multiple choice, short answer, quantitative problem, integrative case).
  3. Identify their weak areas in the Session 1–8 material and have a specific study plan for the next 48 hours.
  4. Construct a well-organized handwritten formula sheet covering the critical material.

19.2 Pre-Class Assignment

  • Review: Sessions 1–8 lecture notes
  • Work: PS1 solutions (released 48 hours after PS1 deadline)
  • Practice: Sample questions from the midterm blueprint
  • Prepare: Bring a draft formula sheet to class for review

    • LA-CAPM
    • Vintage-year effects
  • Speaker notes: This is your study triage. Master Tier 1 first. Get to Tier 2 if time. Tier 3 is bonus.

19.4.4 Slide 9.4 — Worked Example #1: Short Answer Style

  • Title: “Type 1 — Short Answer”
  • Question: “State the GE-LAV stationary distribution of \(L_t\) at calibrated parameters κ = 0.45, σ = 0.32, \(\bar{L} = 1.0\). Show how you compute the variance.”
  • Answer (worked live):
    • Stationary distribution: \(L_\infty \sim \mathcal{N}(\bar{L}, \sigma^2/(2\kappa))\)
    • Mean: 1.0
    • Variance: \(0.32^2 / (2 \times 0.45) = 0.1024 / 0.9 = 0.1138\)
    • Std: \(\sqrt{0.1138} = 0.337\)
    • Distribution: \(L_\infty \sim \mathcal{N}(1.0, 0.1138)\)
  • Common errors to avoid:
    • Reporting \(\sigma\) instead of \(\sigma^2/(2\kappa)\)
    • Forgetting the factor of 2
    • Mixing up variance and standard deviation
  • Speaker notes: Walk through this on the board. Have students write it down.

19.4.5 Slide 9.5 — Worked Example #2: Quantitative Problem

  • Title: “Type 2 — Quantitative Problem”
  • Question: “Compute the Jensen bias for a 15-year infrastructure asset at \(A = 0.18\%\)/yr and \(C = 0\). Express in dollars for a \(\$200M\) DCF mark.”
  • Answer:
    • \(B(T) = A \cdot T + C = 0.18\% \times 15 + 0 = 2.7\%\)
    • Dollar impact: \(\$200M \times 2.7\% = \$5.4M\)
    • LAV mark: \(\$200M + \$5.4M = \$205.4M\)
    • Direction: LAV > DCF (always for Jensen channel)
  • Speaker notes: Make sure students remember Jensen bias is always positive. This catches them.

19.4.6 Slide 9.6 — Worked Example #3: Conceptual Essay

  • Title: “Type 3 — Conceptual Essay (200 words)”
  • Question: “Explain why DCF’s constant-premium assumption fails for private market assets, citing both empirical and theoretical evidence.”
  • Outline of a strong answer:
    • Sentence 1: State the failure precisely (the premium varies stochastically)
    • Sentence 2–3: Empirical evidence (secondary market data 2003–2024; range 0–50%)
    • Sentence 4–5: Theoretical reason (Jensen’s inequality + McKean-Vlasov endogeneity)
    • Sentence 6: Magnitude implication (Jensen bias 1–4%; equilibrium correction up to 70%)
    • Sentence 7: Bottom-line (DCF systematically misvalues; magnitude regime-dependent)
  • Common errors:
    • Listing all three failures without depth on any
    • Forgetting to cite empirical magnitude
    • Confusing Jensen bias with the equilibrium correction
  • Speaker notes: Quality > quantity. A focused 150-word answer beats a sprawling 250-word answer.

19.4.7 Slide 9.7 — Worked Example #4: Integrative Case

  • Title: “Type 4 — Integrative Case”
  • Quick walkthrough of the sample case from the midterm blueprint (the 2007-vintage trapped LP)
  • Key steps:
      1. DCF computation: hold-to-liquidation PV
      1. LAV adjustment using \(r_\text{LAV}(L)\)
      1. Decomposition (level vs. convexity)
      1. Qualitative discussion of additional GE-LAV considerations
  • Time budget on the exam: 30 minutes for this question alone
  • Speaker notes: Have a printed copy of the sample case for students. Work through (a) and (b) live; assign (c) and (d) for self-study.

19.4.8 Slide 9.8 — Formula Sheet: What to Include

  • Title: “Building your formula sheet”
  • Must include:
    • OU SDE: \(dL_t = \kappa(\bar{L} - L_t) dt + \sigma dW_t\)
    • OU stationary: \(L_\infty \sim \mathcal{N}(\bar{L}, \sigma^2/(2\kappa))\)
    • Half-life: \(\ln(2)/\kappa\)
    • Premium function: \(\pi(L) = \pi_0 - \pi_1 L + \pi_2 L^2\)
    • DCF rate decomposition: \(r_f + \beta \cdot \text{ERP} + \text{size} + \pi\)
    • Jensen bias affine form: \(B(T) = AT + C\)
    • Asset class calibration: A values (0.10–0.18%/yr)
    • KS PME: \(\sum D_t/B_t / \sum C_t/B_t\)
    • Five requirements (one-line each)
  • Speaker notes: This is the essential list. Don’t waste your formula sheet on derivations students need to memorize anyway.

19.4.9 Slide 9.9 — Formula Sheet: What to Omit

  • Title: “What NOT to put on your formula sheet”
  • Omit:
    • General CAPM formulas (presumed prerequisite)
    • Detailed derivation steps (not on the midterm)
    • GE-LAV math (Part 2 content; not on midterm)
    • Calibration numbers you should know cold (κ = 0.45, σ = 0.32)
    • Worked examples (use the space for formulas instead)
  • Speaker notes: Density matters. A focused 30-formula sheet beats a chaotic 100-formula sheet.

19.4.10 Slide 9.10 — Study Plan for the Next 48 Hours

  • Title: “Your 48-hour study plan”
  • Day 1 (24 hours before exam):
    • Re-read book Ch. 1 carefully (the conceptual core)
    • Work all PS1 questions a second time
    • Finalize formula sheet
  • Day 2 (12 hours before):
    • Re-read book Ch. 4–5 (measurement + theory)
    • Practice all four sample question types
    • Light review of OU process intuition
  • Last 6 hours:
    • One pass through your formula sheet
    • Light review only — no new content
    • Sleep
  • Speaker notes: Stress the sleep point. Tired students perform worse than students who study less but sleep more.

19.5 Discussion Questions

For Q&A in the last 5 minutes:

  1. What’s the one concept from Sessions 1–8 you’re least confident about?
  2. Which question type do you find hardest — short answer, quantitative, conceptual, or integrative?
  3. What would you do differently if you could re-take Sessions 1–8?

19.6 Common Student Concerns (Instructor Notes)

  • “How much partial credit is given?” Generous on quantitative problems if work is shown. Strict on short answers (right or wrong). Conceptual essays graded against rubric.
  • “What if I freeze on the integrative case?” Start with the easiest sub-part (usually (a)). Work outward. Even partial answers on (b), (c), (d) earn credit.
  • “Can I bring a typed formula sheet?” No — must be handwritten. The act of writing it is part of the study process.
  • “Is GE-LAV math on the midterm?” No. Only intuition and applications. The math is reserved for Track 2 in Sessions 25–31.

19.7 What to Expect Next Session

Session 10 is the midterm exam. Arrive 10 minutes early. Bring all required materials. Power off phones.

After the midterm, Unit 3 begins with exit timing, portfolio construction, regulation, and platform implementation. The shift from “what’s wrong with DCF” to “what does GE-LAV deliver” begins.
